Amaltas

The Golden Shower Tree of India

Cassia fistula

The Amaltas, nature's golden chandelier, transforms Indian summers into a visual symphony. This deciduous marvel grows 10-15m tall, with pinnate leaves that fold up at night in a sleeping posture called nyctinasty.

Beyond its beauty, every part serves a purpose - from its bark used in tanning to flowers that yield yellow dye for monk's robes in Thailand.
